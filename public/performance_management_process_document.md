# Employee Performance Management Platform - Current Process Document

## Project Overview
**Company:** HRTech Corp  
**Project:** Employee Performance Management Platform  
**Current Employee Satisfaction:** 27% (73% find reviews ineffective)  
**Target Employee Satisfaction:** 90%+  
**Employees:** 2,500+ across 8 offices  
**Current Process:** Annual-only, manual reviews  
**Target Process:** Continuous, data-driven performance management

## Current 10-Step Performance Management Process

### Step 1: Annual Review Cycle Initiation
- **Action:** Annual review cycle initiated by HR with email notifications
- **Who:** HR Team, Managers, Employees
- **Duration:** 1-2 weeks
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Annual-only feedback is too infrequent
  - Employees and managers forget about the process
  - No ongoing performance tracking
  - HR has to chase managers for completion
  - No automated reminders or tracking
  - Process feels disconnected from daily work

### Step 2: Manual Form Distribution
- **Action:** Manual distribution of performance review forms (Word documents)
- **Who:** HR Team, Managers
- **Duration:** 1-2 weeks
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Paper-based forms are outdated
  - No standardized format across departments
  - Forms can be lost or misplaced
  - No version control for form updates
  - Manual distribution is time-consuming
  - No tracking of form completion status

### Step 3: Employee Self-Assessment
- **Action:** Employees complete self-assessment forms independently
- **Who:** Employees
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours per employee
- **Real Challenges:**
  - No guidance on how to complete assessments
  - Employees struggle to remember year-old achievements
  - No connection to goals or objectives
  - Subjective self-evaluation
  - No real-time data to support assessment
  - Employees may inflate their performance

### Step 4: Manager Evaluation
- **Action:** Managers complete evaluation forms based on memory and limited notes
- **Who:** Managers
- **Duration:** 3-6 hours per employee
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Managers rely on memory rather than data
  - No ongoing performance tracking
  - Limited notes and documentation
  - Subjective evaluations
  - No standardized evaluation criteria
  - Managers may be biased or inconsistent

### Step 5: HR Compilation
- **Action:** HR compiles completed forms and schedules review meetings
- **Who:** HR Team, Managers, Employees
- **Duration:** 1-2 weeks
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Manual compilation is time-consuming
  - Forms may be incomplete or missing
  - No automated scheduling system
  - Difficult to coordinate multiple schedules
  - No tracking of completion status
  - HR spends more time on admin than strategy

### Step 6: Performance Discussion
- **Action:** One-on-one performance discussions held between manager and employee
- **Who:** Manager, Employee
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Discussions often focus on past rather than future
  - No structured conversation format
  - Managers may not be trained in feedback delivery
  - Employees may be defensive or unprepared
  - No follow-up action items
  - Discussions may be uncomfortable or unproductive

### Step 7: Performance Ratings
- **Action:** Performance ratings assigned using 5-point scale
- **Who:** Manager, HR Team
- **Duration:** 1-2 days
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Subjective rating system
  - No clear criteria for each rating level
  - Ratings may be inflated or deflated
  - No calibration across managers
  - Ratings may not reflect actual performance
  - No connection to business outcomes

### Step 8: Development Planning
- **Action:** Development plans created during review meeting (often generic)
- **Who:** Manager, Employee
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Plans are often generic and not actionable
  - No follow-up on plan execution
  - No connection to career development
  - Plans may not align with business goals
  - No resources or support for development
  - Plans are forgotten after the meeting

### Step 9: Form Filing
- **Action:** Forms filed in employee records with limited follow-up
- **Who:** HR Team
- **Duration:** 1-2 days
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Forms are filed and forgotten
  - No ongoing tracking of development plans
  - No integration with other HR systems
  - No analytics or reporting
  - No follow-up on action items
  - Process feels disconnected from daily work

### Step 10: Separate Compensation Review
- **Action:** Annual salary review process conducted separately by Compensation team
- **Who:** Compensation Team, HR Team
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks
- **Real Challenges:**
  - No connection between performance and compensation
  - Separate processes create confusion
  - No transparency in compensation decisions
  - Employees may not understand the connection
  - No data-driven compensation decisions
  - Process feels disconnected from performance

## Key Pain Points Summary

### Process Inefficiencies
- **Annual-Only Feedback:** Too infrequent for meaningful development
- **Manual Processes:** All steps require manual intervention
- **Paper-Based System:** Outdated forms and documentation
- **No Integration:** Performance management disconnected from other systems
- **Administrative Burden:** HR spends more time on admin than strategy

### Performance Management Issues
- **Subjective Evaluations:** Based on memory rather than data
- **No Ongoing Tracking:** No continuous performance monitoring
- **Generic Development Plans:** Not actionable or specific
- **No Goal Alignment:** Performance not connected to business objectives
- **Poor Follow-Up:** No tracking of development plan execution

### Employee Experience
- **Low Satisfaction:** 73% of employees find reviews ineffective
- **No Real-Time Feedback:** Employees don't know how they're performing
- **Disconnected Process:** Performance management feels separate from daily work
- **No Career Development:** Limited connection to career growth
- **Poor Communication:** Unclear expectations and feedback

### Manager Experience
- **Administrative Burden:** Managers spend time on admin rather than coaching
- **No Training:** Managers not trained in feedback delivery
- **No Tools:** Limited tools and resources for performance management
- **Subjective Process:** No data to support evaluations
- **No Follow-Up:** No tracking of development plan progress

### Business Impact
- **Low Engagement:** Poor performance management impacts employee engagement
- **High Turnover:** Ineffective reviews contribute to employee turnover
- **Poor Performance:** No ongoing performance improvement
- **No Data-Driven Decisions:** Performance decisions not based on data
- **Limited Succession Planning:** No data for talent development
