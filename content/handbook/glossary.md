# Glossary

## A

**Acceptance Criteria**  
Clear, testable conditions that define when a user story or requirement is considered complete and acceptable.

**Agile**  
A set of methodologies for software development that emphasize iterative delivery, collaboration, and responding to change over following a plan.

**Analysis**  
The process of examining and breaking down complex information to understand its structure, relationships, and implications.

## B

**Backlog**  
A prioritized list of features, requirements, or user stories that need to be implemented in a product or project.

**Business Analysis**  
The practice of enabling change in an organizational context by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders.

**Business Process**  
A series of steps or activities that an organization performs to achieve a specific goal or outcome.

## C

**Change Management**  
The discipline of helping individuals, teams, and organizations transition from their current state to a desired future state.

## D

**Data Dictionary**  
A centralized repository that defines and describes the data elements, structures, and relationships used in a system or organization.

## E

**Elicitation**  
The process of gathering information from stakeholders to understand their needs, requirements, and constraints.

**Epic**  
A large user story that represents a significant feature or capability and typically spans multiple iterations or releases.

## F

**Functional Requirement**  
A requirement that describes what a system should do or how it should behave.

## M

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**  
The smallest version of a product that can be released to users while still providing value and allowing for learning.

## N

**Non-functional Requirement**  
A requirement that describes how a system should perform, rather than what it should do (e.g., performance, security, usability).

## S

**Sprint**  
A time-boxed iteration in Scrum, typically lasting 1-4 weeks, during which a development team works to complete a set of user stories.

**Stakeholder**  
Any person, group, or organization that can affect or be affected by a project, product, or business change.
