# Customer Support Ticket Management System - Current Process Document

## Project Overview
**Company:** ServicePro Technologies  
**Project:** Customer Support Ticket Management System  
**Current Resolution Time:** 4.2 days average  
**Target Resolution Time:** 1.5 days  
**Current First-Contact Resolution:** 68%  
**Target First-Contact Resolution:** 85%  
**Monthly Tickets:** 8,000+  
**Active Customers:** 12,000+

## Current 10-Step Support Process

### Step 1: Ticket Submission
- **Action:** Customer submits ticket via email or web form
- **Who:** Customer, Support Team
- **Duration:** Immediate
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Multiple submission channels create confusion
  - No standardized ticket format
  - Customers often provide incomplete information
  - No automated categorization of issues
  - Duplicate tickets created across channels
  - No validation of customer information

### Step 2: Manual Ticket Assignment
- **Action:** Tickets manually assigned to available support agent (first-come, first-served)
- **Who:** Support Manager, Support Agents
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours
- **Real Challenges:**
  - No consideration of agent expertise
  - Uneven workload distribution
  - Agents may not have required skills
  - No prioritization of urgent issues
  - Manual assignment takes time
  - No tracking of assignment decisions

### Step 3: Independent Issue Research
- **Action:** Agent researches issue independently using personal notes and experience
- **Who:** Support Agent
- **Duration:** 2-6 hours
- **Real Challenges:**
  - No centralized knowledge base
  - Agents work in silos
  - Duplicate research across agents
  - No sharing of solutions
  - Personal notes may be outdated
  - No access to previous similar cases

### Step 4: Manual Escalation Process
- **Action:** If escalation needed, agent emails senior team member
- **Who:** Support Agent, Senior Support Staff
- **Duration:** 4-24 hours
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Email escalation is slow
  - No clear escalation criteria
  - Senior staff may be unavailable
  - No tracking of escalation status
  - Customer not informed of escalation
  - No SLA for escalation response

### Step 5: Free-Text Documentation
- **Action:** Resolution attempts documented in free-text fields
- **Who:** Support Agent
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Inconsistent documentation standards
  - No structured format
  - Difficult to search and retrieve
  - No categorization of solutions
  - Documentation quality varies
  - No integration with knowledge base

### Step 6: Manual Customer Updates
- **Action:** Customer updated via manual email responses
- **Who:** Support Agent, Customer
- **Duration:** 2-8 hours
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Email responses are slow
  - No automated status updates
  - Customer may not see updates
  - No tracking of communication
  - Inconsistent communication style
  - No customer self-service options

### Step 7: Ticket Closure
- **Action:** Ticket closed when customer stops responding or confirms resolution
- **Who:** Support Agent, Customer
- **Duration:** 1-3 days
- **Real Challenges:**
  - No clear closure criteria
  - Tickets may be closed prematurely
  - No verification of resolution
  - Customer may not confirm resolution
  - No follow-up process
  - No customer satisfaction measurement

### Step 8: No Knowledge Capture
- **Action:** No systematic knowledge capture or sharing
- **Who:** Support Team
- **Duration:** Ongoing issue
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Solutions not captured for reuse
  - Knowledge lost when agents leave
  - No learning from resolved cases
  - Duplicate work on similar issues
  - No knowledge base maintenance
  - No training material creation

### Step 9: Manual Reporting
- **Action:** Monthly reporting compiled manually from ticket data
- **Who:** Support Manager, Analytics Team
- **Duration:** 3-5 days monthly
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Manual data compilation
  - No real-time reporting
  - Limited analytics capabilities
  - Reports often outdated
  - No automated insights
  - Difficult to identify trends

### Step 10: Volume-Based Performance
- **Action:** Performance reviews based on ticket volume rather than quality metrics
- **Who:** Support Manager, HR Team
- **Duration:** Monthly/Quarterly
- **Real Challenges:**
  - Quality not measured
  - No customer satisfaction metrics
  - Agents focus on quantity over quality
  - No recognition of complex cases
  - No performance improvement feedback
  - No skill development tracking

## Key Pain Points Summary

### Operational Inefficiencies
- **Manual Processes:** All support steps require manual intervention
- **No Intelligent Routing:** Tickets not routed based on expertise
- **Knowledge Silos:** No centralized knowledge management
- **Slow Escalation:** Manual escalation process is time-consuming
- **Poor Documentation:** Inconsistent and unstructured documentation

### Customer Experience Issues
- **Long Resolution Times:** 4.2 days average resolution time
- **Poor Communication:** No automated status updates
- **No Self-Service:** Customers can't check status or resolve issues
- **Inconsistent Service:** Quality varies by agent
- **No Proactive Support:** Reactive rather than proactive approach

### System Limitations
- **No Integration:** Support system doesn't integrate with other systems
- **Limited Analytics:** Poor reporting and analytics capabilities
- **No Automation:** No automated workflows or processes
- **No Knowledge Base:** No centralized knowledge management
- **No SLA Tracking:** No automated SLA monitoring

### Performance Issues
- **Low First-Contact Resolution:** 68% vs industry standard of 85%+
- **High Customer Churn:** 3% increase in churn attributed to support issues
- **Poor Agent Productivity:** Agents spend time on administrative tasks
- **No Quality Metrics:** Performance measured by volume, not quality
- **Limited Scalability:** System can't scale with business growth
