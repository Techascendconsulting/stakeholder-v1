## Lessons (4 Comprehensive Lessons)

### 1. Who Are Stakeholders? (8 min read)
**Topics Covered:**
- What defines a stakeholder
- Why stakeholders matter to BAs
- Types of stakeholders (Internal, External, Primary, Secondary)
- Real-world examples (Food Delivery App Redesign)
- How to identify stakeholders
- Consequences of missing stakeholders
- Building a stakeholder register

**Key Takeaway:** Understanding that stakeholders are the people who make your work possible - without them, you'd be guessing at requirements instead of discovering real business needs.

---

### 2. Stakeholder Analysis: Power and Interest (10 min read)
**Topics Covered:**
- The Power/Interest Grid framework
- Four quadrants explained:
  - Manage Closely (High Power, High Interest)
  - Keep Satisfied (High Power, Low Interest)
  - Keep Informed (Low Power, High Interest)
  - Monitor (Low Power, Low Interest)
- Real-world example (E-commerce Checkout Redesign)
- Step-by-step guide to creating a Power/Interest Grid
- Why stakeholder analysis prevents wasted effort

**Key Takeaway:** You can't give everyone the same level of attention. Prioritize based on who has the most influence and who cares the most.

---

### 3. Communication and Engagement Planning (9 min read)
**Topics Covered:**
- Why communication planning matters
- Communication planning framework:
  - Grouping stakeholders by engagement needs
  - Defining communication objectives (Inform, Consult, Involve, Approve)
  - Choosing the right communication format (1-on-1, Workshops, Email, Dashboards, Town Halls)
  - Setting communication frequency
- Real-world example (Warehouse Management System)
- Creating a stakeholder engagement plan template
- Common mistakes to avoid

**Key Takeaway:** Good BAs don't just gather requirements - they build relationships. The right people need the right information at the right time.

---

### 4. Conducting Stakeholder Interviews (12 min read)
**Topics Covered:**
- Why stakeholder interviews matter
- Types of interviews (Discovery, Requirements, Validation, Design Review)
- How to prepare for an interview
- Conducting the interview:
  - Setting the scene
  - Asking open-ended questions
  - Active listening techniques
  - Capturing key information
  - Closing strong
- Real-world example (Warehouse Receiving Manager interview)
- Common interview mistakes and how to avoid them
- Post-interview follow-up

**Key Takeaway:** Stakeholder interviews are where requirements come from. Documents give you context, but people give you truth.
